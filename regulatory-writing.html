Regulatory Writing

Regulatory writing is a specialized field that involves the development of preclinical and clinical research procedures compiled from clinical trials, safety reports, and other documents to prepare submission-ready documents for the target audience. It is a branch of medical writing in which the medical writer or regulatory clinical medical writer focuses on the documents that need submission to the regulatory authorities at various stages of a trial or study.

The drug development process requires effective planning and strategy before it is ready for launch in the market. The process must go through a thorough evaluation and finally to the consumer. All write-ups must be very precise, must go through a specific process, and be in the provided formats. The role of regulatory writer broadens here who must focus on the documents that are required to be submitted to the regulatory authorities at various stages of a clinical trial.

Regulatory writing is an integral part of clinical research. The essential research work done can be utilized in any clinical or drug development process to improve global health. It can enhance understanding of health and diseases, their management, and can improve medical procedures that can benefit the entire society.

Our Regulatory Writing Service Includes

Clinical study reports (CSRs)

These documents summarize the results of clinical trials and are required for submission to regulatory authorities to support the approval of new drugs and medical devices.

Investigator's brochures (IBs)

These documents provide essential information about the investigational product, including its pharmacology, toxicology, and safety, and are used to support clinical trials.

New drug application (NDAs)

These are comprehensive documents submitted to regulatory authorities to seek approval for a new drug, which includes clinical trial data, manufacturing information, and labeling.

Common Technical Document (CTD)

This is a standardized format for submitting regulatory documents in the pharmaceutical industry. It includes five modules that cover different aspects of drug development, including clinical data, quality, and safety.

Regulatory briefing documents

These are documents that summarize the key information about a drug or medical device to be presented to regulatory authorities to support the approval process.

However, some clinicians and academic scientists cannot be available to write or submit their research work. The other reasons for not publishing may be inadequate data analysis support, lack of awareness of current reporting guidelines and publication practices, or even inadequate technical writing expertise. All these factors result in a poor quality of writing and can have possibilities of high rejection from established peer-reviewed journals or regulatory authorities.

To address these issues, Shanlitics provides support to develop these regulatory writing documents and helps in submission of the applications and ready documents for regulatory approval. Shanlitics provides support in the development of various regulatory documents such as dossier preparation, product registration for local and export purposes, investigator’s brochure, summary of product characteristics (SmPCs), protocols, case report forms, informed consent forms, clinical summaries, safety narratives, etc.

In conclusion, regulatory writing is a crucial aspect of drug development and the submission of documents to regulatory authorities. Shanlitics provides services related to the preparation of regulatory documents, including clinical study reports, investigational new drug applications, and marketing authorization applications. These services are vital to ensure the safety and efficacy of the drug before it is launched and utilized by the consumer, and to fulfill the ethical and moral obligation of publishing research work.
